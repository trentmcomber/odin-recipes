Bourbon Chicken

Description:

Bourbon Chicken is actually named after Bourbon Street in New Orleans, Louisiana, as well as the ingredient that gives it it's bold, boozy flavor!

Ingredients:
  • 1.5lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut in 1in pieces.
  • 2tbsp cornstarch, divided
  • 2tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 1/4 cup minced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1tsp grated fresh ginger
  • 1/3 cup reduced sodium soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup reduced sodium chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup bourbon (can be subbed for apple juice)
  • 3tbsp packed brown sugar
  • 1tbsp cider vinegar
  • 1/4tsp crushed red pepper
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 2 green onions, sliced
Steps:
  1. Toss chicken with 1tbsp cornstarch into a medium bowl and mix
  2. Heat 1bsp oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high heat; add chicken. Cook and stir until browned; transfer to a plate using a slotted spoon.
  3. Heat remaining 1tbsp oil in same skillet. Add onion, garlic, and ginger; cook and stir until softened and fragrant, 1 to 3 minutes.
  4. Stir remaining 1tbsp cornstarch, soy sauce, broth, bourbon, brown sugar, vinegar, and crushed red pepper together in a small bowl.
  5. Add sauce mixture to skillet; cook and stir until thickened and bubbly.
  6. Return chicken to skillet. Cook and stir until coated in sauce and heated through, about 2 minutes.
  7. Serve over rice. Top with green onions.
